About ProSutra

An integrated desktop application for streamlined protein structure prediction and validation.

Introduction

ProSutra is designed to simplify and accelerate the process of protein structure prediction for researchers. By integrating powerful modeling tools and a comprehensive validation suite, it provides a seamless workflow from sequence to structure.

Key Features:

  • Dual Modeling Approaches: Utilizes both homologous modeling with Modeller for template-based predictions and ab initio modeling with Robetta for novel proteins.
  • Comprehensive Validation: Integrates a suite of well-respected validation tools to critically assess model quality.
  • Streamlined Workflow: A user-friendly interface that guides the user from job submission to results analysis.
  • Project-Based Management: Organizes all your modeling and validation runs into clear, manageable projects.

Modeling Methods

Homologous Modeling (Modeller)

Template-based modeling is a powerful technique that relies on the known structure of a homologous protein (a template) to predict the structure of a target protein. This method is highly effective when a template with high sequence similarity is available. ProSutra uses the industry-standard Modeller software to perform this task locally on your machine.

Ab Initio Modeling (Robetta)

For proteins with no known homologous structures, ab initio (or de novo) modeling predicts the structure from the amino acid sequence alone. ProSutra integrates with the Robetta web service, which uses deep learning and the power of Rosetta to generate high-quality de novo models.

Validation Suite

A critical step in protein structure prediction is the validation of the generated models. ProSutra integrates several tools to provide a comprehensive assessment of model quality, including checks on stereochemistry, geometry, and overall fold.

The suite includes:

  • PDBFixer: To repair common issues in PDB files.
  • DSSP: For secondary structure assignment.
  • FreeSASA: To calculate solvent accessible surface area.
  • MolProbity: For detailed geometry and clash score analysis.
  • ProSA: To check the overall quality of the protein fold.
  • VoroMQA: For residue-level quality assessment.
  • QMEAN: A composite score for estimating model quality.
